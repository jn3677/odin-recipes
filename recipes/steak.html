Steak Recipe

Description

Steak, a culinary masterpiece cherished by meat enthusiasts worldwide, embodies the pinnacle of carnivorous indulgence. This revered cut of beef, meticulously selected and expertly prepared, is a celebration of succulence and flavor. Whether it's a ribeye, sirloin, filet mignon, or a strip steak, each boasts its unique texture, marbling, and depth of taste. A perfectly seared steak, its exterior forming a delectable crust while maintaining a juicy interior, delivers a symphony of savory notes. The cooking process, whether grilled, pan-seared, or broiled, unlocks the steak's inherent richness and tenderness, inviting diners to relish the primal pleasure of each mouthwatering bite. Accompanied by complementary sauces or simply adorned with a sprinkle of salt and pepper to let its natural flavors shine, a beautifully cooked steak epitomizes the art of meat craftsmanship, leaving an enduring impression of culinary excellence.

Ingredients

  • ½ cup vegetable oil
  • ⅓ cup low-sodium soy sauce
  • ¼ cup red wine vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons fresh lemon juice
  • 1 ½ tablespoons Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • ½ teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1 (1 1/2-pound) flank steak

Steps

  1. Gather all ingredients.

  2. Whisk together oil, soy sauce, vinegar, lemon juice, Worcestershire sauce, Dijon mustard, garlic, and pepper for marinade until thoroughly combined. Place steak in a 9x13-inch glass baking dish.

  3. Pour marinade over flank steak in the baking dish; turn several times to coat thoroughly with marinade. Cover, and refrigerate for 2 to 6 hours, or up to 12 hours if you have time.

  4. When ready to cook, preheat an outdoor grill for medium-high heat and lightly oil the grate.

  5. Remove steak from the marinade and shake off excess. Discard the remaining marinade.

  6. Cook steak on the preheated grill for about 5 minutes per side, or to desired doneness.

  7. Remove from the grill and let rest for 5 minutes before slicing and serving.

  8. Serve hot and enjoy!
